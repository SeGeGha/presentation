What is "good" code?

Understandability is cool:

  • it’s easier to look for errors
  • it’s easier to change, modify and support

Simplicity => KISS

Clarence Johnson

American aircraft designer (27.02.1910 - 21.12.1990)

If we cannot understand our product - we cannot use it correctly

  • Occam's razor:
    «Entities should not be multiplied without necessity».
  • Leonardo da Vinci:
    «Simplicity is the ultimate sophistication».
  • Ludwig Mies van der Rohe:
    «Less is More».
  • Bjarn Stroustrup:
    «Make simple tasks simple!».

Kiss in programming:

  • no sense to limit a level of abstraction indefinitely, you need to be able to stop in time;
  • no sense to lay in your project unnecessary functions which may in the future and be useful to someone, or may not be;
  • you should not connect a huge library if you only need a couple of functions from it;

Kiss in programming:

  • decomposition of complex things into simple components is an architecturally correct approach;
  • absolute mathematical accuracy or extreme detail is not always needed - most systems are not designed to launch spacecraft, data can and should be processed with the accuracy that is sufficient for a high-quality solution to the problem.

Your strategy:

  • visualize;
  • stabilize;
  • optimize.
